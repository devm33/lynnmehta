It is important to me that my paintings captivate the imagination both up close and from a distance. I draw inspiration from the world around me, finding beauty in the everyday and the extraordinary. <\/em>Elements that spark my creativity include weathered farm buildings, bustling city streets, the gesture of a figure, the vibrant colors of flowers, expansive vistas, misty blue mountains, quiet rivers, and the powerful crash of waves. My work seeks to transform these moments into visual stories that invite viewers to pause and explore.<\/em><\/p>\n

Oil Painter\u00a0<\/em><\/strong><\/h3>\n

Color, composition, and paint handling are key elements in my paintings and tools for my\u00a0expression<\/strong>. The fluidity and flexibility of oil paint is well-suited to my creative process. When I paint I connect with changing light, the color of my surroundings, and movement.<\/strong> I focus on abstract shapes and forms which weave together a whole painting. <\/span>\u00a0<\/span><\/p>\n

Landscape and the Outdoors<\/strong><\/em><\/h3>\n

I grew up along the Pacific Ocean in California and have painted as long as I can remember. I attribute my love of the outdoors and different places to childhood days at the beach, summer backpacking and camping trips, living in India when I was 10 years old, and miles of traveling. Both new and familiar landscapes inspire me.<\/p>\n

My love of art has been my life.<\/strong><\/em><\/h3>\n

I was influenced by the art of my mom who painted the California landscape and encouraged my interest in art by setting up my toddler easel outdoors, enrolling me in art classes, giving me personal instruction, and purchasing art supplies for me. I developed a love for books early and spent hours pouring over the “Art for Children” books by Ernest Lloyd Raboff, especially Pablo Picasso and Paul Klee.<\/span><\/p>\n

In middle school and high school, I took every advanced and honors art class I could fit into my schedule, completely immersing myself in creativity. That passion eventually led me to San Diego State University, where I earned my art degree. Through all of those years, I explored different techniques, styles, and medium, which helped shape both my skills and my perspective as an artist.<\/p>\n

As my children were growing up, I taught part-time at their school, leading the art summer camp and extended day art program. Watching their experience reinforced my focus on the importance of process, and teaching children allowed me to rediscover the joy of learning and exploring through their eyes. My fascination with art and art history inspired me to earn a certificate in World Art from the Smithsonian Associate Program in Washington, DC.<\/p>\n

Professional Artist<\/em><\/strong><\/h3>\n

<\/span><\/p>\n

As a full-time artist I teach classes, workshops, and mentor students.\u00a0 I enjoy engaging in demonstrations, public art talks, and jurying shows.<\/span><\/p>\n

Throughout my career, I have been the recipient of\u00a0 numerous honors and awards. My artwork can be found in public and private collections, the galleries that represent me, and national juried exhibitions.<\/span><\/p>\n

Signature Membership Status I have achieved includes the American Impressionist Society<\/a> and the <\/span>American Society of Marine Artists.<\/a><\/p>\n

I am a juried artist in the\u00a0Oil Painters of America, the Salmagundi Club<\/a>,\u00a0and the\u00a0Washington Society of Landscape Painters.<\/a><\/p>\n

Local organizations I belong to include the Art League of Alexandria in Virginia, Loudon Sketch Club, and the Maryland Federation of Art in Annapolis<\/p>\n

Media highlights include\u00a0a 5-page Artist Article in the well circulated Plein Air Magazine<\/a>,<\/em>\u00a0 an Artist Profile in Washington, DC’s\u00a0Hill Rag<\/a>\u00a0<\/em>and I am one of the artists of the\u00a0100 Plein Air Painters of the Mid-Atlantic<\/a><\/em>.<\/p>\n
