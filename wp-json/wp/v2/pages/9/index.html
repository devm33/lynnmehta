It is important for me to create a painting which is interesting both up close and further away in order to engage the imagination. Inspiration is something which is honest to me and something which I believe makes a universal connection.<\/h4>\n

Oil Painter\u00a0<\/em><\/strong><\/h3>\n

Color, composition, and paint handling are key elements in my paintings and tools for my\u00a0expression<\/strong>. The fluidity and flexibility of oil paint is well-suited to my creative process. When I paint I connect with changing light, the color of my surroundings, and movement.<\/strong> I focus on abstract shapes and forms creating a whole. <\/span>\u00a0<\/span><\/p>\n

Landscape and the Outdoors<\/strong><\/em><\/h3>\n

Painting outdoors in Virginia<\/p><\/div>\n

I live in Alexandria, Virginia, along\u00a0the Potomac River which flows into the Chesapeake Bay and the Atlantic Ocean. I grew up along the Pacific Ocean, in La Jolla, California and have painted as long as I can remember. I attribute my love of the outdoors and different places to childhood days at the beach, summer backpacking and camping trips, living in India when I was 10 years old, and miles of traveling. Both new and familiar landscapes inspire me. Beaches, cities, mountains, farms, rivers, deserts, docks trigger my imagination.<\/p>\n

<\/h3>\n

<\/h3>\n

My love of art has been my life.<\/strong><\/em><\/h3>\n

I was influenced by the art of my mom who painted the California landscape and encouraged my interest in art by setting up my toddler easel outdoors, enrolling me in art classes, giving me personal instruction, and purchasing art supplies for me. I developed a love for books early and spent hours pouring over the “Art for Children” books by Ernest Lloyd Raboff, especially Pablo Picasso and Paul Klee.<\/span><\/p>\n

As my own children were growing up, I taught part-time at their school, heading up the Art Summer Camp and Extended Day Art Program. I hold a\u00a0 Certificate in World Art from the Smithsonian Associate Program in Washington, DC and continue to take advantage of the art history courses the Smithsonian offers.<\/p>\n

Professional Artist<\/em><\/strong><\/h3>\n

Discussing painting – Best Use of Light – Mountain Maryland<\/p><\/div>\n

As a full-time artist I teach classes and workshops, offer demonstrations and artist talks to the public, and jury shows. I have received numerous honors, awards, and professional accolades. I sell my work in multiple galleries and events. My paintings are internationally collected.\u00a0<\/span><\/p>\n

I hold Signature Membership Status in the American Impressionist Society<\/a>, the <\/span>American Society of Marine Artists,<\/a>\u00a0 and the Mid-Atlantic Plein Air Painters Association.<\/span><\/p>\n

I am a juried artist in the\u00a0Oil Painters of America, the Salmagundi Club<\/a>,\u00a0and the\u00a0Washington Society of Landscape Painters.<\/a>.<\/p>\n

Other memberships which I maintain include the Art League of Alexandria in Virginia and the Maryland Federation of Art in Annapolis.<\/p>\n

I serve on the board of the Mid-Atlantic Plein Air Painters Association and am the Regional Eastern Representative for the American Society of Marine Artists.<\/p>\n

Media highlights include\u00a0a 5-page Artist Article in the well circulated Plein Air Magazine<\/a>,<\/em>\u00a0 an Artist Profile in Washington, DC’s\u00a0Hill Rag<\/a>\u00a0<\/em>and I am one of the artists of the\u00a0100 Plein Air Painters of the Mid-Atlantic<\/a><\/em>.<\/p>\n
